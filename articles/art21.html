The National Zoo’s Panda Cub Has a Name: Xiao Qi Ji

The cub had a live birth and a social media following — everything, it seemed, but a name. Until now. By Christine Hauser Three months after his birth, the National Zoo’s panda cub now has a name. Welcome Xiao Qi Ji, a Mandarin phrase that translates to “little miracle” in English, the zoo announced on Monday. The name was one of four that the Smithsonian’s National Zoo and Conservation Biology Institute asked the public to choose from for the cub, who was born on Aug. 21 at the zoo’s David M. Rubenstein Family Giant Panda Habitat.
