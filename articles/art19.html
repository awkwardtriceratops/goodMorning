What Happened When a School District Banned Thin Blue Line Flags

Some students in a mostly white New York suburb said the flag made them feel unsafe. Banning it prompted accusations of political bias. By Michael Gold In late October, administrators in a suburban New York school district told employees that some of their apparel was making students feel uncomfortable, and even threatened. At issue were masks showing the so-called thin blue line flag, which signals support for the police but which has increasingly been used to display opposition to the Black Lives Matter movement, which rose in opposition to racism in policing.
