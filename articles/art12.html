The Pandemic Economy in 7 Numbers

Some data portray hope: Spending on consumer goods is higher than it was before the pandemic, and the housing market is booming. Others paint a more dire picture: The service sector is struggling badly, and the federal aid that initially saved the economy from ruin could soon disappear. Using seven key numbers, we look at the sectors that have been affected most profoundly and consider what the path to recovery might look like. Ben Casselman, who covers economics and business for The New York Times, walks us through the pandemic’s impact.
