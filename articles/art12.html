The Supreme Court Should Not Muck Around in State Election Laws

It is dangerous and destabilizing. By Akhil Reed Amar, Vikram David Amar and Neal Kumar Katyal The authors are law professors. Just as they did in the infamous Bush v. Gore litigation in 2000, Republican lawyers are trying to get the Supreme Court to undermine state court rulings protecting voting rights under state law. Their theory? That state courts, by relying in part on state constitutions, are wrongly exercising power that belongs to state legislatures.
