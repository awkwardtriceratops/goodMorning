The California Wildfires in Pictures

Supported by By The New York Times Thousands of people were forced to flee their homes in Vacaville, a city of about 100,000 residents near Sacramento, as a combination of uncontrolled fires northwest of the city became an increasing threat on Tuesday. The group of fires, known together as the LNU Lightning Complex, has grown to cover more than 32,000 acres and is completely uncontained, the authorities said. Firefighters had hoped for calmer weather overnight, but gusty winds arrived instead, pushing the fire toward vegetation and other fresh fuel, said Lynnette Round, a spokeswoman for California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection, or Cal Fire. Gov. Gavin Newsom declared a state of emergency Tuesday in response to the fires, which have hit the state as it battles the effects of a sweltering heat wave, rolling blackouts and the coronavirus pandemic. The governor also mobilized the California National Guard to assist with relief efforts. Matt Nichols tried to save his home as the LNU Lightning Complex fire tore through an area near Vacaville, Calif., on Wednesday. Over the past week, portions of Monterey, Nevada and Napa Counties have declared mandatory emergency evacuations for thousands of residents. Dozens of fires are burning out of control throughout Northern California. A mobile home was destroyed in Napa County, Calif., on Tuesday.
