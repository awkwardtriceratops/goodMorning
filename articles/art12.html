With Kids in Sports Bubbles, These Parents Are Desperate for a Way In

Carl Tuch quarantined in Las Vegas at the house of his son, Alex Tuch of the Golden Knights, in hopes of being allowed to go watch him play in the N.H.L. playoffs.Credit...Bridget Bennett for The New York Times Supported by By Kevin Armstrong Carl Tuch, an electrical engineer, woke up on Labor Day, grabbed his two suitcases and hopped on a 6 a.m. flight from Syracuse to Las Vegas ready to gamble. His son, Alex Tuch, a right wing on the Vegas Golden Knights, was the one with the hot hand. He had scored eight goals over the previous month en route to the Western Conference finals in Edmonton, Alberta.
