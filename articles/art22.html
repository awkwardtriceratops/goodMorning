Hurricane Laura Live Updates: Storm Strengthens to Category 1

The Gulf Coast was largely spared by Tropical Storm Marco, but officials warned that Laura is expected to be much more damaging. RIGHT NOW Coastal areas in Louisiana and Texas are issuing evacuation orders in preparation for Laura, which may become a major Category 3 hurricane before making landfall late Wednesday or early Thursday. Laura strengthened from a tropical storm into a Category 1 hurricane on Tuesday morning as it cut through the Gulf of Mexico on a path toward the Louisiana coast, where officials have ordered evacuations stretching into Texas. Forecasters said that Laura could become a major Category 3 hurricane late Wednesday or early Thursday as it gathers energy from the Gulf’s warm waters. Gov. John Bel Edwards of Louisiana compared the storm to Hurricane Rita, which caused an estimated $25.2 billion in damage when it struck the state’s coast in 2005.
