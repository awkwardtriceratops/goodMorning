The California Wildfires in Pictures

Supported by By The New York Times Thousands of people were forced to flee their homes in Vacaville, a city of about 100,000 residents near Sacramento, as a combination of uncontrolled fires continued to threaten Northern California on Wednesday. That group of fires, known together as the LNU Lightning Complex, has destroyed more than 50 buildings or structures and is threatening nearly 2,000 more, the authorities said. The fire spread more than 14,000 acres overnight and now covers 46,225 acres in Napa and Sonoma Counties — an area larger than the size of Washington, D.C. — and is completely uncontained, according to California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection. Four people have been injured in the fire. Gov. Gavin Newsom declared a state of emergency Tuesday in response to the fires, which have hit the state as it battles the effects of a sweltering heat wave, rolling blackouts and the coronavirus pandemic. The governor also mobilized the California National Guard to assist with relief efforts. Scorched earth in Vacaville, Calif., after the LNU Lightning Complex tore through the area on Wednesday. Over the past week, portions of Monterey, Nevada and Napa Counties have declared mandatory emergency evacuations for thousands of residents. Firefighters battled to get control of the blazes, which have forced a growing number of evacuations and were likely caused by lightning strikes in recent days. Dozens of fires are burning out of control throughout Northern California. A mobile home was destroyed in Napa County, Calif., on Tuesday.
