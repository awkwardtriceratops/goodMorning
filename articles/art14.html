Clare Bronfman Is Sentenced to 81 Months in Nxivm ‘Sex Cult’ Case

Ms. Bronfman, a Seagram’s heir, played a crucial role in the group, using her family fortune to intimidate critics as it faced allegations that it was an abusive cult, prosecutors say. By Nicole Hong When Clare Bronfman discovered a self-help group called Nxivm in 2003, she was struggling with social anxiety, unable to accept her identity as the daughter of a famous billionaire. Nxivm gave her a sense of purpose, she wrote in court papers. In the course of the next 15 years, she became part of the group’s executive board, even as Nxivm faced mounting criticism that it was an abusive cult that coerced women into sexual slavery. Tapping her fortune, Ms. Bronfman unleashed an army of lawyers and investigators to intimidate Nxivm’s critics.
