Military Surveillance Planes Didn’t Spy on Protesters, Pentagon Report Finds

But the Air Force’s inspector general concluded that National Guard officials failed to obtain prior approval from Defense Secretary Mark T. Esper to use the planes. By Eric Schmitt WASHINGTON — Deploying a little-known National Guard reconnaissance plane in four American cities to monitor protests this spring did not violate rules against the military collecting intelligence on citizens, a Pentagon report has concluded. But the report by the Air Force inspector general found that National Guard officials failed to obtain prior approval from Defense Secretary Mark T. Esper to use the planes because they mistakenly believed they were not intelligence aircraft, which require high-level signoff.
