Daniel Prude’s Death: Police Silence and Accusations of a Cover-Up

The police in Rochester, N.Y., treated the death of Mr. Prude as a drug overdose. The case drew scrutiny when footage showed that he had been pinned down and hooded by officers. By Michael Wilson, Jesse McKinley, Luis Ferré-Sadurní, Troy Closson and Sarah Maslin Nir In the minutes after Daniel Prude’s heart briefly stopped during a struggle with officers who had pulled a hood over his head, an unofficial police narrative took hold: He had suffered a drug overdose. That account hardened when the police chief in Rochester, N.Y., told the mayor that a man in custody was in the hospital after taking PCP, or angel dust.
