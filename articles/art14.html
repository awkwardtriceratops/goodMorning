This Is How Bloomberg Can Help Biden Win Florida

Rather than spend his money on TV ads, Michael Bloomberg can re-enfranchise thousands of formerly incarcerated people in the state by paying off debts tied to their sentences. By Robert Montoye Dr. Montoye is a computer scientist. Those of us who can’t tolerate the thought of another four years of this administration are extremely grateful to Michael Bloomberg for his commitment to spend at least $100 million, primarily on television and digital ads, to increase voter turnout in Florida. But an even more politically effective, and charitable, use of those dollars might be to help pay off the debts of Floridians who have financial obligations related to a felony conviction — as LeBron James and the group behind More Than a Vote did this summer. Because of an 11th Circuit appeals court ruling on Sept. 11, an estimated 774,000 Floridians who have already served their time in jail or prison are not eligible to vote in the 2020 election until they pay the fines and fees associated with their sentences.
