Amy Coney Barrett Is No Ruth Bader Ginsburg

When it comes to the Supreme Court, we’ve been to this identity politics movie before. By Lara Bazelon Ms. Bazelon is a law professor. President Trump’s promise to name a woman to replace Ruth Bader Ginsburg, who died on Sept. 18 at 87, was cynical and insulting to the millions of women who view the late Supreme Court justice as a feminist icon. Senator John Barrasso, a Wyoming Republican, praised the decision as a “powerful positive statement” to young women, who would embrace the president’s nominee as a “role model.” The message to women is clear: Nothing to see here, ladies! One of you is as good as any other.
