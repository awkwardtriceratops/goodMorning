Lockdown Feels Pretty Different the Second Time Around

Israel has become the first country to reimpose a full national lockdown. This one is nothing like the first. By Ruth Margalit Ms. Margalit is a writer. TEL AVIV — When Israel imposed a coronavirus lockdown in March, I walked home after raiding the supermarket and was able to hear the birds chirping on Dizengoff Street, one of the busiest arteries here. The next day I spoke to my father in Jerusalem, where the country’s first death from coronavirus had just been recorded. We both danced around the fact that, since his age made him more susceptible to complications from the virus, it would probably be a long time before we could see each other.
