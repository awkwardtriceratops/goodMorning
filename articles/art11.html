For Trump, It’s Not the United States, It’s Red and Blue States

NEWS ANALYSIS A statement by the president this week was an insight into how he views the country as composed of parts that either are for him or against him. By Peter Baker WASHINGTON — President Trump argued this week that the death toll from the coronavirus was actually not so bad. All you had to do was not count states that voted for Democrats. “If you take the blue states out,” he said, “we’re at a level that I don’t think anybody in the world would be at. We’re really at a very low level.”
