The Pandemic Brought Loss. Now They’re Behind on Rent.

THE NEEDIEST CASES FUND For these families in Queens, bills piled up. “All this hit and it’s hard because you feel so powerless over everything.” By Emily Palmer The last day Maria Quezada saw her husband alive, he was lying in a hospital bed in Nassau County, his face bloodied after 17 days of intubation. “His face, that’s what hurt me most, seeing how he had been hurting so long — how he suffered,” she recalled in Spanish. “I will never forget his precious face.” Covered in protective gear, Ms. Quezada, who had recently had Covid-19, reached for his hands. They already felt cold.
