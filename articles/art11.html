Don’t Shame Your Neighbors

It’s a favorite American pastime. But it’s not helping. By Annalee Newitz Contributing Opinion Writer During the early months of the Covid-19 pandemic in the United States, Americans were bombarded with contradictory information. Don’t wear masks! Actually, no, scratch that — wear them all the time! Stay in the house! Go outside! Get tested! Don’t get tested! Confused, afraid and desperate to survive, we turned to one of our favorite pastimes: public shaming.
