Top Editor at Los Angeles Times, Norman Pearlstine, Steps Aside

Mr. Pearlstine came out of retirement two years ago to steady the newspaper, one of the country’s largest, after years of turmoil under its previous owner. By Katie Robertson The veteran editor Norman Pearlstine came out of retirement a little more than two years ago to bring stability to The Los Angeles Times. On Monday, less than two weeks after the paper published an article headlined “L.A. Times shaken by a summer of turmoil and scandals,” he told the staff that he was stepping down.
