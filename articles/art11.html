Why I’ll Never Stop Being a Theater Critic

TIMES INSIDER After 27 years on the job, the writer Ben Brantley bids farewell with one last recommendation: Watch a show as if you were a reviewer. By Ben Brantley Times Insider explains who we are and what we do, and delivers behind-the-scenes insights into how our journalism comes together. “Don’t you ever want to just sit back and enjoy it?” That’s a question I’ve often been asked during my 27 years as a daily theater reviewer for The New York Times. And now that I’m leaving that position, it comes up more frequently than ever, with the implication that I must be panting to be able to watch plays without having to think about them so hard.
