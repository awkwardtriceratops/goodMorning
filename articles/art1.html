What to Know About Bird-Feeders

Along with bread-baking, board games, gardening and Zoom, bird feeding is enjoying a pandemic uptick. Here’s how to make the most of winter, peak bird-feeding season → Know that timing is everything. Bird feeders can be used year-round, but warm weather increases the risk of transmitting infections like salmonella. In areas with black bears, feed from around Thanksgiving to sometime in March. Think about the location of your feeder. Impact with window glass is a leading cause of death for birds. Position feeders either within three feet of windows, or farther than 30 feet away. No perch should be so close that squirrels can leap from a branch onto the feeder. Feeders on a pole with a sleeve-like baffle that slides up and down can defy climbing squirrels or raccoons. What’s on the menu? Sunflower and suet are favorites. Peanuts delight woodpeckers and sparrows. Finches love thistle. Project FeederWatch at the Cornell Lab of Ornithology offers help based on species and region. Birds are also thirsty. In cold weather, water may be scarcer than food. A floating de-icer can keep water in bird baths or garden ponds open. A heated pet bowl works too.
