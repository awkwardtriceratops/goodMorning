Is Ivanka Trump Going to Change Anyone’s Mind?

Four years ago, the would-be first daughter was seen by some to have the power to persuade moderate women voters and to rein in her own father’s impulses. A lot is different now. By Elaina Plott On night four of the Republican National Convention, viewers will know her as “The Honorable Ivanka Trump.” The title is a nod to her senior role in the White House (though official titular etiquette is somewhat unclear). The speaking gig is not. While the White House insists she is appearing “on her own time,” and only in her capacity as President Trump’s eldest daughter, Ms. Trump will introduce the president on Thursday evening before he addresses the convention from the White House lawn, an overtly political use of federal property and potential violation of the Hatch Act.
