Bryson DeChambeau Tames Course and Doubters With U.S. Open Win

U.S. OPEN DeChambeau, who bulked up and went all-in to prove a power game could flout golf’s norms, shot the only under-par round on Sunday to win the 2020 U.S. Open. By Bill Pennington MAMARONECK, N.Y. — Bryson DeChambeau won the 2020 U.S. Open by six strokes as power trumped accuracy at Winged Foot Golf Club. He bettered 21-year-old Matthew Wolff to win the tournament, which was held without fans, on Sunday. DeChambeau shot the day’s only under-par round, a 3-under 67, and celebrated winning his first major tournament, pumping both arms enthusiastically after sinking his putt on the 18th green. Greeted with a video call from his parents at the clubhouse, DeChambeau choked back tears as the moment settled in. Since, he routinely out-drives competitors by 50 yards or more on some holes. This is developing news.
