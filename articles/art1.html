Are Republican Judges Putting Their Thumbs on the Electoral Scale?

Recent decisions certainly suggest as much. By Erwin Chemerinsky Mr. Chemerinsky is dean of the law school at the University of California, Berkeley. President Trump and his Republican followers believe they can ensure victory in November by limiting the number of people who can vote. What’s worse, in recent decisions, Republican-appointed judges seem to have fallen in step with this strategy, more inclined to advance the party’s interests than uphold the Constitution.
