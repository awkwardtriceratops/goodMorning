Ben Cross, Star of ‘Chariots of Fire,’ Dies at 72

“The whole thing about acting is that you draw on other people’s experiences,” he said. By Azi Paybarah Ben Cross, the actor best known to one generation for playing a determined runner in the Academy Award-winning film “Chariots of Fire” and to another audience decades later for his role in a reboot of “Star Trek,” died on Tuesday after a short illness. He was 72. His daughter Lauren announced his death, after an unspecified illness, on Mr. Cross’s Facebook page. She said he had died in Vienna, The Associated Press reported.
