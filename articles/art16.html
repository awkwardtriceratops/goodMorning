Europe’s Economic Revival Is Imperiled, Raising the Specter of a Grinding Downturn

NEWS ANALYSIS As the coronavirus regains force, economists fear that Europe’s tentative recovery is at risk from traditional political concerns. By Peter S. Goodman and Liz Alderman LONDON — Europe was supposedly done with political histrionics. In the face of the pandemic, a continent not known for common purpose had put aside long-festering national suspicions to forge a collective economic rescue, raising hopes that a sustainable recovery was underway.
