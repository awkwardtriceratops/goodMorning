By Jesse McKinley ALBANY, N.Y. — Battered by a unrelenting pandemic and a crippled economy, New Yorkers experienced an Election Day like no other in the state’s history. New York, as expected, quickly awarded its 29 electoral votes to the Democratic candidate, the former Vice President Joseph R. Biden Jr., despite President Trump’s long association with the state, both as a Queens native and a headline-grabbing builder in New York City.
