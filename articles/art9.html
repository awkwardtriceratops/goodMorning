Family of Lauren McCluskey Agrees to $13.5 Million Settlement in Daughter’s Death

The University of Utah, where Ms. McCluskey, 21, was killed in 2018, said its employees had “failed to fully understand and respond appropriately to Lauren’s situation.” By Christine Hauser The state of Utah has agreed to pay more than $10 million to the family of Lauren McCluskey, a University of Utah student whose nude photographs were shared by a campus police officer from whom she had sought help before she was murdered in 2018, according to a settlement and court documents. The university “acknowledges that the murder of Lauren McCluskey was a brutal, senseless and preventable tragedy,” the agreement said.
