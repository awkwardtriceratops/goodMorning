Mali’s Ousted President Is Released From Detention by Coup Leaders

Ibrahim Boubacar Keïta was allowed to return to his home in the capital by the military officers who forced him to resign as president. By Ruth Maclean and Cheick Amadou Diouara DAKAR, Senegal — Ibrahim Boubacar Keïta, Mali’s ousted president, was released from detention and driven to his home in the West African country’s capital early Thursday, nine days after a military coup brought an end to his seven years in power. A heavy security escort took Mr. Keïta to his house in Sebeninkoro, a residential neighborhood of Bamako, capping his abrupt fall after military officers with guns arrested him, took him to their base and forced him to resign on television.
