Shopping for Blankets

SHOPPING GUIDE Because it may not be cold yet, but it will be soon. By Tim McKeough As evenings turn colder, blankets are useful just about everywhere: by the fire, in front of the television, on top of the bed. But they work well as decorative accents at almost any time of year — especially when they have vibrant colors and patterns. A large-scale textile like a blanket “can add a lot of interest to a bedroom,” said Katie Ridder, the New York-based interior designer. “Most of the space is usually taken up by the bed, so it’s a nice place to have beautiful textiles.” Ms. Ridder changes blankets with the seasons — “Cotton for the summer and wool for the winter,” she said — often using them to add a punch of color to a room.
