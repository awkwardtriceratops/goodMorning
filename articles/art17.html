Shinzo Abe Is Quitting, and Leaving a Trail of Scandals Behind

Will he ever face the Japanese people’s calls for accountability? By Koichi Nakano Mr. Nakano is a political scientist in Japan. TOKYO — Prime Minister Shinzo Abe’s announcement on Friday that he would resign because of poor health was a rather abrupt end for a supposedly strong leader. Mr. Abe has ruled Japan, most recently, for a record seven years and eight months: He is the country’s longest-serving prime minister.
