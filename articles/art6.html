Now the President and Frontline Workers Have Something in Common

Farmworkers in April in California.Credit...Brent Stirton/Getty Images Supported by By Jeneen Interlandi Ms. Interlandi is a member of the editorial board. Most of the women in Maria Jose Bastias’s English language class, at Mujeres Unidas y Activas, a nonprofit in San Francisco, did not know that they could get a coronavirus test without health insurance, or that they were legally entitled to paid sick leave if they contracted the virus, regardless of their immigration status. They worked grueling hours at menial jobs that offered little security; it had not occurred to many of them to even ask about such benefits, let alone to expect them. But they all had heard about what happened at the nearby McDonald’s: Employees there asked their boss for face masks to wear at work, and were given dog diapers and coffee filters to use instead.
