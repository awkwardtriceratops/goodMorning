Authentic Wins the Kentucky Derby, Run Without Fans

The race in Louisville ran with a smaller field than usual, no spectators and despite protests nearby over the police killing of Breonna Taylor in the city earlier this year. By Joe Drape LOUISVILLE, Ky. — There will be no Triple Crown bid in this strangest of horse racing seasons. Authentic upset Tiz the Law, the Belmont champ, to capture America’s most famous race, this year contested in the midst of the coronavirus pandemic that has altered the whole of the sporting world.
