Biden’s Cabinet Picks, Part 1: Janet Yellen

Ms. Yellen, however, is no stranger to crisis or political machinations. She served as a top economic adviser in the Clinton White House. She was among the first economists to spot the housing bubble. And as chairwoman of the Federal Reserve from 2014 to 2018, she helped navigate the country through the last major financial emergency. Now, facing another steep challenge, we review her history-making career and look at the measures she might take to get the economy humming again. Jeanna Smialek, who covers the Federal Reserve and the economy for The New York Times. Ms. Yellen became an economist when few women entered the discipline. She is now set to become the first female Treasury secretary and one of the few people ever to have wielded economic power from the White House, the Federal Reserve and the president’s cabinet.
