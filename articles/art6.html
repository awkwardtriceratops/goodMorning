Coronavirus Crisis Shatters India’s Big Dreams

A fabric manufacturing business in Surat, India, has been shut since the coronavirus lockdown in the spring.Credit...Saumya Khandelwal for The New York Times Supported by By Jeffrey Gettleman SURAT, India — The hit that India’s dreams have taken from the coronavirus pandemic can be found in the hushed streets of Surat’s industrial zone. You can see it in textile mills that took generations to build but are now sputtering, eking out about a tenth of the fabric they used to make. You can see it in the lean faces of the families who used to sew the finishing touches on saris but, with so little business, are now cutting back on vegetables and milk. You can see it in the empty barbershops and mobile phone stores, which shoppers have deserted as their meager savings dwindle to nothing. Ashish Gujarati, the head of a textile association in this commercial hub on India’s west coast, stood in front of a deserted factory with a shellshocked look on his face and pointed up the road. “You see that smokestack?” he asked. “There used to be smoke coming out of it.” CHINA PAKISTAN New Delhi INDIA GUJARAT Surat Mumbai Bay of Bengal 200 MILES
