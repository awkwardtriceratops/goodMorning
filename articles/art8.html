In Maine, a Race About the Issues Has Become a Referendum on Susan Collins

Her opponent, Sara Gideon, is running on her record in the state — but does it matter in a year when all politics is national? By Sarah Lyall Two years ago Sara Gideon, the Democratic speaker of the Maine House of Representatives, announced that she was sick and tired of the partisan rancor permeating her state’s government. So she ripped up the seating chart in the 151-member chamber, the one where Republicans and Democrats traditionally glare at each other from across the room, and mixed up the two parties.
