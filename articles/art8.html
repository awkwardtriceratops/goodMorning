In Praise of PBS, a True Democratic Institution

Next month will mark 50 years of television that aims to educate and unite. By Margaret Renkl Contributing Opinion Writer NASHVILLE — On Oct. 4, 1970, the Public Broadcasting Service entered the airwaves with an episode of “The French Chef.” I was not quite 9, too young for Julia Child and too old for “Sesame Street.” PBS became the television constant of my life anyway.
