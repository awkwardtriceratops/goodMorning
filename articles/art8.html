The Venezuelan-American Vote, From a First-Timer in Florida

Our vote could play a key role in November. We need to see beyond the bluster, and look at the way Trump has treated our community in the U.S. By Mariana Atencio Ms. Atencio is a journalist. MIAMI — This year I’ll be voting for the first time in a U.S. presidential election. Registering to vote online, the option to mail-in my ballot or vote early — it’s all new to me. The last time I voted, in 2013 in Caracas, Venezuela, I lined up for hours under the blazing hot sun, surrounded by heavily armed soldiers, to cast my vote against Nicolás Maduro. After depositing my ballot in a box, I dipped my pinkie finger in purple ink, visible proof that I’d voted.
