Ruth Falcon, Soprano Turned Master Teacher, Dies at 77

Ms. Falcon had a career singing at major opera houses before becoming a much sought-after teacher. Her students included Deborah Voigt and Nadine Sierra. By Anthony Tommasini Ruth Falcon, a soprano who sang leading roles at major international opera houses and went on to become a sought-after voice teacher, mentoring prominent artists including Deborah Voigt, Sondra Radvanovsky and Danielle de Niese, died on Oct. 9 in Manhattan. She was 77.
