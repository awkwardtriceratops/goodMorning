The Real Divide in America Is Between Political Junkies and Everyone Else

Most Americans view politics as two camps bickering endlessly and fruitlessly over unimportant issues. By Yanna Krupnikov and John Barry Ryan Ms. Krupnikov and Mr. Ryan teach political science at Stony Brook University. The common view of American politics today is of a clamorous divide between Democrats and Republicans, an unyielding, inevitable clash of harsh partisan polarization.
