Kenzo Takada, Who Brought Japanese Fashion to the World, Dies at 81

He planned to stay in Paris for six months but stayed 56 years, becoming known for bold, exuberant prints. He died of the novel coronavirus. By Vanessa Friedman Kenzo Takada, the designer whose exuberant prints helped bring Japanese fashion to the world, died on Sunday at a hospital in Paris. He was 81.
