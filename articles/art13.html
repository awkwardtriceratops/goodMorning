Why Trump’s Taxes Are a Call to Action

For a more equitable system, we need a simpler and fairer tax code. By Veronique de Rugy Ms. de Rugy is a senior research fellow at the Mercatus Center at George Mason University. When Americans recently learned how little President Trump has paid in taxes in the past 15 years and how he benefited from financial maneuvers, it reinforced the widespread belief that the rich don’t pay their fair share. Lost in the outrage is the fact that the tax provisions that allowed Mr. Trump to trim his tax bill were probably not illegal or the results of tax schemes concocted by anti-tax legislators.
