Fox News Meets Trump’s Fraud Claims With Skepticism

Supported by Even “Fox & Friends,” a warm venue for the president, sounded doubts about his talk of a stolen election. CNN and MSNBC dismissed the claims. By Tiffany Hsu and John Koblin Nearly a decade ago, Donald J. Trump had a regular Monday slot on the Fox News morning show “Fox & Friends,” and it has been a reliably friendly venue for him throughout his presidency. But more recently, the show has been cool to his unsubstantiated claims of widespread vote fraud. On Thursday’s episode, the co-host Steve Doocy challenged Pam Bondi, a former attorney general of Florida and a Trump supporter, over her comment about “fake ballots that are coming in late.” “Pam, did you just say fake ballots?” Mr. Doocy asked. “There could be. That’s the problem,” Ms. Bondi replied.
