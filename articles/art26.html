Stimulus, Trump Taxes, Twitter: Your Friday Evening Briefing

Here’s what you need to know at the end of the day. By Victoria Shannon and Karen Cetinkaya (Want to get this newsletter in your inbox? Here’s the sign-up.) Good evening. Here’s the latest. 1. President Trump is holding a rally on the White House lawn tomorrow, his first public appearance since he returned home on Monday from Walter Reed National Military Medical Center for treatment for the coronavirus. Trailing Joe Biden in the polls, the president had originally hoped to travel to in-person rallies this weekend. He is now also planning to attend an event in Florida on Monday. Mr. Biden, shown above at his own campaign stop in Las Vegas today, said only an “overwhelming” election win could counter Mr. Trump’s efforts to undermine trust in the election results. The White House, seeking to revive stimulus talks that Mr. Trump called off just days ago, is preparing a $1.8 trillion economic relief proposal. But the prospect of a compromise with Democratic lawmakers before the election remains remote.
