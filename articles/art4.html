I Asked Bernie Sanders if It Was All Over. ‘No,’ He Groaned.

The senator from Vermont explains why the left still has a fighting chance. By Elizabeth Bruenig Ms. Bruenig is an Opinion writer. I have said goodbye to Bernie Sanders before. There was the parting pageantry of the 2016 Democratic Convention where Mr. Sanders conceded the nomination to Hillary Clinton, closing out a primary season so tight it had given even the most pessimistic of leftists a thrill of hope. As he called for unity against Donald Trump in his farewell, the news network’s camera found a crying 22-year-old Bernie delegate in a Robin Hood hat, and the internet tore him apart. Laugh it up, I thought; there will be a next time.
