Finally, Some Students Return to New York City’s Classrooms

Up to 90,000 children in pre-K and students with advanced disabilities went back to in-person school on Monday. By Eliza Shapiro One principal in Brooklyn has pinned pink heart balloons to the school’s front door to welcome about 50 pre-K children who are expected to arrive for classes. Another principal in Washington Heights is expecting only five children to attend. New York City’s roughly 1,400 school buildings have sat largely empty for six months, since its school system, the nation’s largest, abruptly shuttered classrooms in mid-March to help slow the spread of the coronavirus.
