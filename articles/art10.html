‘It’s Like Playing With Kobe’: An Unlikely Intergenerational Bridge Team

Some of world’s best bridge players are in their 70s. They joined a teenagers’ team for an online tournament. By Abby Ellin One afternoon during the height of coronavirus lockdowns, 16-year-old Finn Kolesnik found himself in a cold sweat. He was on the phone with Bob Hamman, 82, one of the greatest bridge players ever. Kolesnik wanted to know if, um, maybe Hamman and his longtime bridge partner, Peter Weichsel, 77, would join his team for this year’s North American Online Bridge Championships Premier Knockout, being held from July 23 to Aug. 2?
