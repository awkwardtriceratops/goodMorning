Barack Obama Delivers a Jeremiad

In his convention speech, the former president sought to renew faith in the democratic project. By David W. Blight Mr. Blight is a professor of history at Yale and the author of “Frederick Douglass: Prophet of Freedom.” On Wednesday, Aug. 19, 2020, standing in front of an exhibition about the Constitution in Philadelphia, Barack Obama fully became an American Jeremiah. Unlike that Hebrew prophet, Mr. Obama did not shatter the earth, nor predict the destruction of all our temples, nor see our Jerusalem quite yet in its deserved ruin. He did not tell us that collectively our “clothing is stained with the blood of the innocent and the poor,” as Jeremiah did. But he came close, even as he delivered a moving reassertion of American “ideals” and “creeds.”
