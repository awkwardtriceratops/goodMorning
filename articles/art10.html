With whom will we share our secrets now?Credit...Photo by Chris Maggio for The New York Times Supported by By Reyhan Harmanci As the saying goes, if you don’t have something nice to say about anybody, come sit by me. Seriously, sit by me. Like Alice Roosevelt Longworth — to whom that quote has been attributed, and Olympia Dukakis’s Clairee in “Steel Magnolias,” who famously repeats it — I have a weakness for juicy, interpersonal information, a predilection that has followed me into my professional life. Not that I’m alone. Researchers define gossip as “talking about someone who is not present” and, according to a 2019 meta-analysis, people spend an average of 52 minutes a day doing it.
