Stream These Vintage Heist Movies

Some of the most popular heist films of the last 25 years are remakes of beloved classics. Here’s where you can get away with streaming the originals, plus some other vintage-cool crooks ➡ ‘The Italian Job’ (1969) A Cockney thief (Michael Caine) and his comrades steal gold from gangsters, leading to one of the most famous car chases in cinema history — and a slick 2003 remake. Where to stream: Amazon Prime ‘The Thomas Crown Affair’ (1968) Few onscreen couples have smoldered as convincingly as this millionaire playboy bank robber and the insurance investigator who might be onto him. Where to stream: Apple TV+ and more ‘Ocean’s 11’ (1960) Before George Clooney tried robbing a Las Vegas casino onscreen, Frank Sinatra did it first, vaulting Sinatra and the Rat Pack into the Hollywood firmament. Where to stream: Google Play and more ‘The Sting’ (1973) Cue “The Entertainer,” the piano accompaniment to a classic pairing. Where to stream: Peacock and more ‘Le Cercle Rouge’ (1970) “France was the home of cool, midcentury crime pictures, and no Frenchman did them better” than this film’s director, Jean-Pierre Melville, wrote The Times’s Jason Bailey. Three trenchcoated tough guys are out to rip off a jewelry store with a police inspector hot on their trail.
