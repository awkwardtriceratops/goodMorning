E.U. Trade Chief Quits, a Casualty of Dinner That Flouted Virus Rules

Phil Hogan had one of the most powerful jobs in Brussels, and leaves at a pivotal time in trade relations. The dinner he attended with politicians in Ireland has become a political scandal. By Steven Erlanger and Matina Stevis-Gridneff BRUSSELS — Phil Hogan, the influential trade commissioner for the European Union, resigned Wednesday night over breaches to coronavirus guidelines during a recent dinner with lawmakers and other public figures in his native Ireland. Mr. Hogan issued a statement expressing regret and making an “apology to the Irish people.’’
