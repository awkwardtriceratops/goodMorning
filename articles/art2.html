New York City’s 3 Percent Problem

A single number — the percentage of positive coronavirus tests across the city over a seven-day period — would dictate whether public schools would close their doors. The figure hit the threshold last week. There are questions, however, around whether closing was necessary — some public health maintain that the city’s schools are safe. When is the right time for schools to reopen and what is the right threshold for closing? We explore what lessons New York City’s struggles hold for the rest of the nation. Eliza Shapiro, who covers New York City education for The New York Times, walks us through the city’s decision to reopen schools and the difficult decision to shut them down.
